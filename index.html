Seb McKenzie

Founder & CEO of SmarterCash

Just over 8 months ago, I was ready to sign up for welfare. Last week I flew to LAX to collect the keys for my new place.

Many of my friends, shocked, have congratulated me on my hard work - except it wasn’t hard work. It was smart work.

My name is Seb McKenzie.

I, like most people, was not born into great wealth or opportunity. I come from a small town in Leeds, England, with a housekeeping mother and door-to-door salesman father. I had little-to-no platform for any kind of success on the scale that I wanted. I was frustrated for years.

I had passion. I had creativity. Above average intelligence, with a below average income. So much potential, but such few opportunities. I was wondering what I’d done wrong, and why that financial freedom seemed so unreachable. Sound familiar?

After countless ‘get rich quick’ schemes, surveys, saving tips, I learned there’s no such thing as easy money. Anything that offers quick cash must have a catch. The rich and successful did not become rich and successful by taking shortcuts and playing it safe. They got there because they had an idea, believed in it, and went in with 120%.

You may feel like you’ve been there before. Giving your all with nothing in return. That’s because everyone is taught to “put in the grind, then reap the rewards”, “work hard and you’ll succeed”.

What they don’t often emphasise is the key ingredient: Having a defined vision.

I can’t stress enough the importance of having clear picture of what you’re doing, and where you want to be. People can’t invest in you unless they know your intentions and what you stand for. If your vision is ‘do anything that will make me rich’, you are not investible and offer nothing of value. Everyone wants to be rich.
